February & March 2017

Since I didn't make a post in February due to some heavy courseloads, I decided that this month I'd include what I've been up to since my last post.

February

Late at the end of January and into early February, two of my friends and myself participated in the Boilermake IV hackathon. What came out of thise coding-packed weekend was a webapp that will crawl through President Obama's WhiteHouse.gov and President Trump's WhiteHouse.gov and compare the differences between the two, hoping to show the different policy positions that each President took/has taken thus far.

We call this project Diff-Prez, and you can check out our progress via our public repository here.

In addition to Diff-Prez, I spend a good amount of my time this month working on a projcect for my Systems Programming course, in which we used Lex and Yacc to make an interpreter for a scripting language that my professor created. We also created our own parsing grammar, added bracketed lists, and gave it the ability to define any extra functions that the user may need.

And last but not least, I have accepted an internship offer with ECS Solutions, a batch-automation company located in Evansville, IN. I am extremely excited to work for them this upcoming summer and gain invaluable industry experience!

March

The month of March is still unraveling itself, but that hasn't kept me from staying busy. This month a majority of my time has been devoted to the Shell Project, a decade old project in my Systems Programming course that has been quite the challenge. We were to create a functioning terminal shell for UNIX machines, complete with extra features like Wildcarding, command history, environment variables, and much more.

Once I have completed my coursework at Purdue, I will be sure to upload it as well as my other computer science coursework to my GitHub so that I may keep a public record of what I have worked on over the years.

In addition to the Shell, I am beginning to work on the ideas behind a small project for iOS and/or macOS. I want to make a application that allows for each course schedule planning for High School/University courses. This could allow them to plan their days/weeks in a timetable, along with information like the course name, course numbers, instructors, and more.
